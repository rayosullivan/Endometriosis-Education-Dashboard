Project Title

EndoPath: A Digital Endometriosis Triage, Education and Care-Navigation Platform

Lead Applicant

Prof. Ray O’Sullivan

Clinical Academic, Women’s Health and Fertility

Project Duration

12 months

Funding Requested

To be finalised within CIF envelope (€50,000–€200,000)

Background and Rationale

Endometriosis is a chronic, inflammatory gynaecological condition affecting approximately 1 in 10 women of reproductive age. Despite its prevalence, diagnostic delay remains unacceptably prolonged, typically ranging from 7–9 years, with significant consequences for physical health, mental wellbeing, workforce participation, and health-system efficiency. In Ireland, patients commonly experience fragmented care pathways, repeated GP consultations, inappropriate reassurance, and delayed access to specialist services.

There is currently no standardised digital triage or education pathway embedded within Irish public healthcare to support early identification, prioritisation, and patient understanding of endometriosis.

Innovation Description

EndoPath is a clinician-designed digital platform that delivers:

Structured symptom-based triage

Evidence-based patient education aligned with ESHRE and national guidance

Risk stratification to support appropriate referral prioritisation

Outputs designed to integrate with Irish public outpatient workflows

The platform has already been prototyped and demonstrated, positioning this project as a translational service-delivery innovation rather than speculative development.

Model of Care Impact

EndoPath introduces a redesigned outpatient access pathway, shifting from reactive referral-based care to proactive, structured triage and education. This enables earlier identification of high-risk patients, reduces inappropriate investigations, and improves clinic efficiency.

Anticipated Outcomes

Reduced time from first presentation to specialist review

Improved patient-reported understanding and preparedness

Improved quality and appropriateness of referrals

Reduced outpatient congestion and inefficiency

Strategic Alignment

The project aligns with Sláintecare principles, the HSE Digital Health Strategy, and national priorities in women’s health and integrated care.

Sustainability

Once embedded, the platform will operate as part of routine care with minimal ongoing cost and clear potential for spread to other HSE sites.

3. CIF-Aligned Application Narrative
Clinical Problem

Women with suspected endometriosis experience prolonged delays in diagnosis and access to specialist care, resulting in repeated consultations, psychological distress, and inefficient use of healthcare resources. Existing pathways rely heavily on unstructured referrals and inconsistent patient education.

Proposed Solution

EndoPath provides a digital triage and education platform that standardises early assessment, empowers patients with accurate information, and supports clinicians in prioritising referrals based on symptom complexity and risk.

Innovation and Added Value

This project represents a clear service-delivery innovation by redesigning the outpatient access pathway. It is neither a routine IT procurement nor a research registry. The innovation lies in the integration of structured triage, education, and care navigation into a single, clinician-designed digital workflow.

Implementation Plan

Refinement of existing prototype for HSE use

Clinical validation of educational content

Pilot deployment within an Irish public service

Measurement of predefined patient, staff, and system outcomes

Outcomes and Evaluation

Evaluation will focus on measurable indicators including referral quality, patient-reported understanding, and time to specialist review, assessed at 6 and 12 months.

Risk and Mitigation

Key risks relate to adoption and integration, mitigated through clinician leadership, limited scope, and alignment with existing pathways.

Sustainability and Spread

The platform will be embedded into routine practice and is scalable to other sites and related chronic pelvic pain pathways.
